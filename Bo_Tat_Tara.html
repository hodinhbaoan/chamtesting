Bodhisattva Tara Statue

8th Century | Bronze | Champa Culture

A National Treasure of Vietnam

The bronze statue of Bodhisattva Tara, recognized as a National Treasure of Vietnam in 2013, is one of the most exquisite Champa sculptures ever discovered. It represents the goddess of compassion in Mahayana Buddhism, radiating serenity and divine grace.

Discovery and Craftsmanship

Unearthed in the Mỹ Sơn Sanctuary in 1978, this masterpiece is 1.13 meters tall and weighs 65 kilograms. Crafted from high-quality bronze, it showcases delicate jewelry, a tall crown, and flowing robes — clear evidence of advanced metallurgical skill during the 8th century.

Spiritual Significance

The Tara statue is a rare example of female imagery in Cham art. Her posture exudes confidence and divinity, with one hand raised in a gesture of protection. The statue reveals how Hindu-Buddhist beliefs coexisted in Champa spiritual life.
